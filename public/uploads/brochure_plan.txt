 For Italy, Japan, Korea, Spain, etc., don't fake product photos. Instead, design each country as a visual tile: a styled composition of a few evocative stock/licensed images — a Tokyo street at dusk, a Kyoto tea ceremony, sakura, a bowl of ramen — overlaid with a short list of "what's inside" in elegant typography. It reads as intentional editorial design, not as "we don't have the product yet." Think travel magazine, not Amazon listing. You can even frame it as a feature: "Each box is a journey. Here's where this one takes you.”

(About page 9 / 10) The one exception: future country boxes themselves are absolutely worth flagging as "coming soon" because that creates anticipation within your signature line and gives HRs a reason to check back. "Japan — arriving July 2026" is exciting.
